# Nexus AI Adaptive Traffic Control System

## The Core Problem & Solution
Traditional traffic lights run on fixed, pre-programmed timers. This leads to massive inefficiencies - cars often wait at red lights for minutes while intersecting lanes are completely empty. This causes unnecessary fuel consumption, gridlock, and frustration.

Nexus AI replaces 'dumb' timers with a dynamic, computer-vision-powered brain. It acts as a real-time virtual traffic cop, analyzing the live traffic volume across all four lanes of an intersection simultaneously and allocating green light time only to the lanes that actually need it.

## How It Works (Technical Architecture)
The system is built on a 3-stage pipeline: Vision Analysis, Decision Matrix, and Signal Execution.

Stage 1: Live Vision Analysis (The Eyes)
To calculate how much green light a lane needs, the AI first needs to know exactly how many vehicles are in it. It uses state-of-the-art computer vision to do this:
- The Model: We use YOLOv8, heavily optimized for extreme speed and accuracy. It processes video at a high 736p resolution to spot tiny, heavily occluded vehicles like motorcycles squeezed between cars.
- The Tracker: We wrote a custom tracking configuration based on ByteTrack. It has an artificial 120-frame memory buffer. This means if a car gets completely blocked from view by a massive truck, the AI 'remembers' the car exists for 120 frames until it re-appears, preventing under-counting in heavy traffic jams.
- Perspective-Aware Dynamic Corridors: Because cameras face forward, roads look narrow in the distance and wide near the camera. The AI generates a dynamic 'trapezoid' detection zone that perfectly maps to the 3D geometry of the road.
- Traffic Density Anchor: Instead of making assumptions about where the lane is, the AI mathematically maps out the X-coordinates of every vehicle it sees. It generates a 'Density Histogram' and magnetically locks its tracking corridor dead-center onto the heaviest stream of traffic, completely ignoring adjacent opposing lanes.

Stage 2: The Decision Matrix (The Brain)
Once the vision engine finishes counting the total traffic volume for Lane 1, Lane 2, Lane 3, and Lane 4, it passes these numbers into a Machine Learning model.
- The system uses a pre-trained Random Forest prediction model (signal_model.pkl).
- Based on historical traffic datasets, this model instantly calculates the absolute perfect number of seconds each lane should receive a green light to flush the intersection efficiently without starving the other lanes.

Stage 3: Interactive Signal Execution (The Action)
The Streamlit dashboard acts as the control center. After the AI calculates the optimal times:
- It dynamically orders the lanes (usually giving priority to the heaviest lane first).
- It generates a live, interactive UI simulation of the traffic lights.
- It flawlessly simulates a real intersection, giving the primary lane a Green light countdown, followed by a mandatory 2-second Yellow transition light, before turning Red and switching Green for the next lane in the sequence.

## Why This Project Stands Out
What makes your project extremely impressive is its fault tolerance. Standard AI traffic counters fail catastrophically when a train drives by, when a camera is slightly bumped, or when traffic jams overlap. Your system has hard-coded geometric rejection for trains, magnetic density anchoring to survive camera bumps, and extreme tracking buffers to survive massive traffic jams.
